Audit Finding — Post-Booking Lifecycle

The Leak Nobody's Talking About

The ads work. The landing page converts. Parker closes. But between "demo booked" and "demo attended" there are four structural failures that are costing PodPitch show rate, conversion rate, and Parker's time.

5 emails audited 1 missing email identified 1 qualification gate missing Jean Mundabi Fala — Growth Lead Candidate
The Root Cause

The SDR goes into every demo blind. The prospect is asked to fix that.

Current Flow
Prospect books → generic confirmation → homework form (paste your own bio) → Lazar goes in blind → prospect no-shows because they forgot why they booked
Fixed Flow
Prospect books → email captured → Clay enrichment runs automatically → Lazar gets a brief → segment-specific confirmation email → prospect feels known → show rate goes up
The Enrichment Layer
1

Booking form captures: first name, last name, email, company name (two extra fields, added before the Cal.com embed)

2

On booking: Clay or Apollo lookup triggered automatically on email and domain — returns title, company size, LinkedIn, industry

3

Prospect auto-assigned to a segment: Agency (managing 3 or more clients) / Founder / In-house PR team

4

SDR brief sent to Lazar: who they are, estimated use case, LTV tier, one personalisation hook

5

Confirmation email pulled from segment-specific template. Prospect reads: "I've done my homework on you." Not: "Please do our homework for us."

1
Pre-Call Email — Sent at Booking

The confirmation that leads with a threat

Sent immediately after booking
Current
Subject: Why You Should Cancel Before Monday, Munya

"Munya: if you think you might not be able to make the Google Meet on Monday, please reschedule ahead of time. People who miss the call without notice are not eligible to sign up for PodPitch."

Here are reasons you might want to cancel our meeting: 1. You're not ready to get press right away. 2. You think PodPitch costs under $100/mo...

Opens with a threat. Loss frame. Makes the prospect feel tested before they've seen a single feature. A warm lead just went cold.
Rewrite
Subject: Your demo Monday — here's what we'll cover, Munya

Hey Munya,

Looking forward to Monday.

I had a look at what you're building before our call. Based on that, I'll focus on how [founders in your space] are getting booked on [relevant podcast type] right now — and what their first 30 days of results look like.

You don't need to prepare anything. Just show up.

If something comes up, reschedule here: [link]. No problem.

See you Monday.
Lazar

Warmth instead of threat. Signals research was done. Removes all friction. Reschedule link framed as low-pressure, not punitive.

Missing Email — 24 Hours Before the Call

The highest-leverage email that doesn't exist

Should be sent 24 hours before the demo
Currently Missing From Sequence
What Happens Now

Nothing. The sequence jumps from the booking confirmation directly to the no-show recovery email.

A prospect who booked 5 days ago and has since gone cold receives zero communication before the call. No reminder. No anticipation. No reason to still show up.

The 24-hour reminder is the single highest-leverage touchpoint in the demo lifecycle. It is the last chance to re-confirm intent. It does not exist.
New Email to Add
Subject: Tomorrow at [time] — one thing to know first

Hey Munya,

Quick note before tomorrow.

[Founders in your position] get the most out of the demo when we focus on one thing: the first 10 podcast interviews. Not a full strategy — just the first 10.

That's what we'll build together tomorrow in real time. By the end of the call you'll have a list, a pitch angle, and a realistic timeline.

See you at [time].
Lazar

P.S. Here's 90 seconds from [similar user] on what their first 30 days looked like: [link]

Creates anticipation for a specific outcome. Social proof from someone in the same situation. Makes the prospect want to attend — not just remember to.
2
No-Show Recovery — Email 1

The missed call email that could be sent to anyone

Sent same day, ~30 minutes after missed call
Current
Subject: (no subject)

"Am bummed we didn't get to connect today — I imagine something came up, no worries at all!"

"With the time back, I pulled some stats from our users: Landing 6+ podcast interviews monthly (3x more than traditional outreach). Saving 13+ hours weekly. Generating $22K+ in advertising value."

Stats apply to everyone. "I imagine something came up" is filler. "With the time back I pulled some stats" reads like an automated trigger — because it is.
Rewrite
Subject: Missed you today, Munya

Hey Munya,

Something came up — happens to everyone.

Before our call I looked at [their company / LinkedIn]. You're at the stage where [specific observation about their situation]. That's usually where PodPitch creates the most obvious before/after.

Worth rescheduling?

[Single reschedule link]

Lazar

One specific observation about their actual situation instead of generic stats. One CTA. Proves research was done — gives the prospect a reason to believe the demo will be worth their time.

No-Show Recovery — Email 2

The feature list that says nothing about the person

Sent next day
Current
Subject: (no subject)

"Hey Munya, Still want to see how PodPitch: Build targeted podcast lists in under 2 mins (vs. 3 hours manually). Generates personalized pitches for each show instantly. Tracks and analyzes pitch effectiveness."

"If scaling your podcast outreach efforts is still relevant, click here to re-schedule!"

Feature list. No reference to Munya's situation. "If scaling your podcast outreach efforts is still relevant" is passive. Could be sent to any of PodPitch's 3,091 users.
Rewrite
Subject: One thing I noticed, Munya

Hey Munya,

I checked [their LinkedIn / company] while prepping for our call.

[Specific finding — e.g., "You've published consistently on LinkedIn but I don't see podcast presence. That's where the biggest distribution gap usually is."]

That's the gap [similar user] closed in 6 weeks — went from 0 to 7 confirmed bookings.

If that gap is real for you: [reschedule link]

Lazar

Leads with a specific finding from their situation. Uses a real before/after from a similar user. Conditional CTA — "if that gap is real for you" — creates higher-quality replies.

No-Show Recovery — Email 3

The same ask, repeated a third time

Sent 4 days after missed call
Current
Subject: (no subject)

"Hey Munya, On average, users save 10-30 hours a week by using PodPitch to generate pitches for them."

"If you still want a personalized demo, grab a new time here. If this isn't relevant right now, is there a better time I should follow-up?"

Same stats for the third email. No escalation. "If this isn't relevant right now, is there a better time I should follow-up?" gives an easy exit without offering an easier entry point.
Rewrite
Subject: Lower-friction option, Munya

Hey Munya,

If scheduling a call is the blocker — no problem.

You can start a 2-week trial now and explore at your own pace: [trial link]

If you'd still prefer the demo first, the calendar's open: [reschedule link]

Either works.

Lazar

Offers a lower-friction alternative (trial) instead of the same high-friction ask three times. Short. Two options. Prospects who skip the demo and start the trial still enter the conversion funnel.
Landing Page Fix

The qualification gate that should exist before the booking CTA

Right now anyone can book. Parker closes. That means demo time is spent on people who cannot afford the product or are not ready to buy. Two fields added before the Cal.com embed fixes this without killing conversion.

Field 1

Who are you booking a demo for? — Yourself / A client / My team

Field 2

What's your company or brand name?

Two fields. Gives the SDR a company name for enrichment even if the email is a personal Gmail. Segments the prospect before the call. Filters window-shoppers. Signals to the prospect that the demo will be personalised — which increases show rate on its own.
