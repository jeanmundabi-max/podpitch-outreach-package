The Core Leak

PodPitch’s comparison page positions the product against MuckRack, Cision, Rephonic, and PodMatch — the platforms PR agencies already know. That makes sense as a starting point. But it means the page was built to convert people who already use those platforms, not people actively searching for a podcast PR tool.

Podseeker ranks in the top results for “podcast PR tool” on Google. PodPitch does not appear. That is not a product problem — it is a comparison content problem. Buyers who find Podseeker first evaluate it first. The comparison page that would redirect those buyers to PodPitch does not exist.

On the other end of the market, Kitcaster charges $2,100 per month for three podcast bookings. That pricing creates a clear population of buyers who want podcast PR but cannot justify the spend — the exact segment PodPitch is built for. No page exists to capture them at the point they decide Kitcaster is too expensive.

The gap is not product. The gap is the comparison layer that reaches buyers already evaluating — and the agency acquisition path that does not yet exist.
What This Seems to Indicate

Podseeker owns the high intent search traffic PodPitch is not capturing

The query “podcast PR tool” is the highest intent search in the category. Buyers typing it are ready to evaluate. Podseeker appears. PodPitch does not. A dedicated comparison page — PodPitch versus Podseeker, feature by feature and outcome by outcome — captures that traffic and redirects it into the PodPitch trial funnel.

Kitcaster is overcharging PodPitch’s ideal customers

$2,100 per month for three podcast bookings positions Kitcaster as a premium concierge service, not a scalable platform. PR agencies managing ten or more clients cannot use it. The buyers who leave Kitcaster because of price have nowhere to land on PodPitch’s site right now. A Kitcaster comparison page captures that movement at the exact moment of decision.

The agency segment is converting without a dedicated acquisition path

Rob, Sierra at Helios Worldwide, and Laurenne are all agency customers. Their outcomes — hours saved, bookings generated, clients served — are not on an agency page. The proof that closes agency buyers is not reaching more of them. A segment page built from those testimonials turns individual conversions into a repeatable acquisition motion.

3,091 PR professionals on the platform. Near zero independent reviews

G2 and Capterra show virtually no PodPitch reviews. A platform used by over three thousand PR professionals and two thousand five hundred founders is essentially invisible on the review sites enterprise buyers check during vendor evaluation. The social proof programme has not been built. It is not a satisfaction problem — it is a systematic ask problem.
